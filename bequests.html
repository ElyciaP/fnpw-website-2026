Leave a gift in your Will

A gift that lasts longer than a lifetime.

A bequest to FNPW is one of the most meaningful gifts you can make. It allows you to protect the wild places you love, for the people who come after you.

your legacyprotected forever
Why leave a bequest

For some, the land is family.

Many of our most significant conservation outcomes, like the 1,900 hectares of Mundoo Island secured for Coorong National Park in 2025, were made possible by the generosity of Australians who included FNPW in their Will.

A bequest costs nothing today. It doesn’t affect your loved ones’ inheritance. And it gives you the satisfaction of knowing that the wild Australia you love will be protected long after your lifetime.

Types of bequest

Three ways to give.

Example

Residuary bequest

Leave a percentage of the remainder of your estate after other gifts have been made. This protects against inflation and is the most common form of bequest.

Example

Pecuniary bequest

Leave a specific sum of money. Straightforward and easy to include in your Will alongside gifts to family and friends.

Example

Specific bequest

Leave a particular item or asset such as property, shares or artwork. We’ll work with your executors to honour your wishes.

Suggested wording

Wording for your Will.

Your solicitor will guide you, but the following wording is a good starting point for including FNPW in your Will:

“I give to the Foundation for National Parks & Wildlife (ABN 51 248 905 949) [the residue of my estate / a sum of $X / X% of my estate] for its general purposes. The receipt of the Chief Executive Officer or other authorised officer shall be sufficient discharge.”

If you’d like to direct your gift to a specific program such as Growing Parks, Saving Species or Healing Our Land, please contact our bequests team. We’ll help you craft language that honours your intent.

Frequently asked

Questions about bequests.

Do I need to tell FNPW if I include you in my Will?
You’re not required to, but we’d love to know. Letting us know allows us to thank you in your lifetime, invite you to our Habitat Heroes events, and ensure your gift is used as you intended. Your decision remains confidential and you can change it at any time.
Can I direct my bequest to a specific project or species?
Yes. While most bequests are made for general purposes, which gives FNPW the flexibility to allocate funding where it’s most needed, but you can also direct your gift to a specific pillar (Growing Parks, Saving Species, Healing Our Land) or program. We recommend discussing this with our bequests team first.
Is my bequest tax effective?
Yes. As a Deductible Gift Recipient, gifts to FNPW are generally exempt from capital gains tax and reduce your estate’s taxable value. Please consult your solicitor or accountant for advice specific to your circumstances.
What if my circumstances change?
You can change your Will at any time. A bequest to FNPW does not lock you in. It is simply an instruction that takes effect when you no longer need your assets.
How will my gift be acknowledged?
With your permission, we honour bequest givers through our Habitat Heroes recognition program, including private events, named project recognition where appropriate, and personal thank-you communications. Many of our donors prefer anonymity, which we always respect.
