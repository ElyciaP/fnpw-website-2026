Who we are

For 55 years, we’ve helped Australia’s wild places endure.

Founded in 1970, FNPW is now an independent national conservation organisation guided by science, strategic partnership and a clear understanding of risk.

Our story

A foundation built on partnership.

FNPW was established on 29 June 1970 by the Hon. Tom Lewis AO as the fundraising arm of the NSW National Parks & Wildlife Service. Today we’re a national conservation organisation focusing where intervention can shift long-term ecological trajectories: securing land for permanent protection, restoring degraded landscapes at scale, and supporting threatened species before decline becomes irreversible.

Acknowledgement of Country

We acknowledge the Traditional Custodians of Country throughout Australia.

We pay our respects to Elders past and present, and honour the knowledge and traditions passed down through generations. Our work is grounded in the understanding that the lands and waters we protect have been cared for by First Nations peoples for tens of thousands of years.

How we deliver impact

Three interconnected pillars.

Growing Parks

We enable the secure expansion of protected areas, ensuring landscapes of high biodiversity value are conserved and actively managed in perpetuity.

Saving Species

Science-led recovery programs that address key threats, improve animal welfare, and strengthen long-term survival through on-ground action and community involvement.

Healing Our Land

Through large-scale restoration, community nurseries and climate-adaptive planting, we help ecosystems recover, adapt and reconnect fragmented habitat.

Reconciliation Action Plan

Our commitment to reconciliation.

FNPW acknowledges that Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples are the Traditional Custodians of lands, seas, waters, flora and fauna, possessing crucial knowledge that supports our shared efforts to achieve a sustainable and healthy country. Our vision is to create such country, led and driven by First Nations peoples, through fostering deep engagement with one of the oldest living cultures on earth; a culture that remains a shining example of community and environmental sustainability.

Our Innovate RAP mirrors our ongoing commitment to strengthen relationships between Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples and non-Indigenous Australians, and to ensure our policies, processes and programs are culturally appropriate and accountable. We are determined to advance reconciliation through meaningful partnerships and continual learning, building a unified Australia where First Peoples’ histories, cultures and expertise are celebrated to drive self-determination and a sustainable future for all.

Download our RAP

Relationships

Building genuine partnerships with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities, prioritising First Nations-owned and operated organisations, businesses and individuals across our programs, procurement and commissioning.

Respect

Proactively seeking free, prior and informed consent from Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, acknowledging cultural heritages, methods and relationships to Country in all activities that may impact upon them.

Opportunities

Implementing a strategic funding matrix that prioritises First Nations organisations, employment and commissioning, ensuring meaningful participation and self-determination across FNPW’s sphere of influence.

Founder & Patron

The late Hon. Tom Lewis AO

As NSW lands minister, Tom Lewis set up the National Parks and Wildlife Service in 1967, then established the Foundation for National Parks & Wildlife in 1970 as its fundraising arm. Today the Foundation he started is an apolitical, independent conservation organisation working Australia-wide.
