Why now

Every year, the bush gets quieter.

Invasive species, climate change and urban development have been giving the bush a short back and sides for decades, clearing the understory, shaving the creek banks and putting pressure on some of our most vulnerable animals.

Australia has lost more mammals to extinction than any other continent on Earth. Every year the bush gets quieter, we edge closer to a future of silence.

But the bush is resilient. And thanks to people like you, the Foundation for National Parks & Wildlife is already growing it back, loud and proud.

More than 29,000 hectares of protected land secured. 110,000 fire-resilient natives propagated and planted. And a future restored for the Growling Grass Frog, once at risk of being lost forever.

So let’s embrace the wild look. Because Australia looks better with a full bush. It always has. And we’re not ready to say goodbye to that just yet.
